# METRICS

The North Star metric for this product is:

## Qualified Audit Completions

A qualified audit completion means:
- The user finishes the audit
- Inputs meaningful spend data
- Receives recommendations
- Has estimated savings potential above a defined threshold

This metric matters more than raw traffic because the product is fundamentally a B2B lead-generation engine. A small number of highly qualified audits is more valuable than thousands of casual visitors.

Three important input metrics drive this North Star metric:

1. Audit completion rate
If users abandon the form early, the funnel breaks before value is delivered.

2. Share rate of audit reports
This measures whether the product is compelling enough to spread organically.

3. Email capture conversion rate
The audit only becomes commercially valuable when users opt into follow-up or consultation.

The first analytics I would instrument are:
- Funnel drop-off by form step
- Time-to-completion
- Savings ranges generated
- Share button clicks
- Consultation CTA clicks

These metrics would reveal whether users trust the recommendations and whether the generated value feels meaningful.

The key pivot threshold would be consultation intent.

If fewer than 3–5% of completed audits result in consultation requests after sufficient traffic volume, it likely means either:
- The savings recommendations are not compelling enough
- The audience targeting is wrong
- Users do not trust the recommendations

At that point, I would revisit either the audit logic or the distribution strategy before scaling acquisition.
