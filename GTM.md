# GTM

The ideal target user for SpendWise AI is an engineering manager or technical founder at a startup with 5–50 employees already spending heavily on AI tooling without clear visibility into optimization opportunities.

These users are usually scaling quickly and have accumulated multiple overlapping AI subscriptions across engineering, research, and content teams. They are technical enough to understand tooling costs but too busy to manually compare plans across vendors.

The moment they need this tool is usually after one of three triggers:
- Their monthly AI bill unexpectedly increases
- A teammate asks for another AI subscription
- They see discussions online about “AI tool stack costs”

Right before using the product, they are likely searching:
- “best AI coding tools for startups”
- “Cursor vs Copilot pricing”
- “reduce OpenAI API costs”
- “AI tooling stack for engineering teams”

They also spend time in:
- r/startups
- r/LocalLLaMA
- Indie Hackers
- Hacker News
- AI engineering Discord communities
- X/Twitter founder circles
- Product Hunt discussions

To get the first 100 users with a $0 paid budget, I would focus on high-signal founder communities instead of broad marketing.

First, I would create short teardown-style posts on X showing real examples of overspending:
“Startup paying $900/month for tools they barely use.”

Second, I would post comparison screenshots and benchmark insights on Reddit communities where AI tool pricing discussions already happen naturally.

Third, I would directly message early-stage founders and developer agency owners offering free spend audits in exchange for feedback.

I would also create a lightweight “AI Spend Calculator” embed for blogs and newsletters focused on AI productivity tooling. This creates distribution through creators already discussing AI workflows.

The unfair advantage Credex has is access to discounted infrastructure credits. Most audit tools can only identify overspending. Credex can actually solve the problem immediately by reducing spend through discounted credits.

That makes the funnel unusually strong:
discover overspend → receive recommendations → convert through Credex.

Week-1 traction success metrics would look like:
- 1,000 landing page visitors
- 200 completed audits
- 40 captured emails
- 10 consultation requests
- 2–3 high-intent enterprise leads

For a B2B infrastructure product, even a handful of qualified enterprise leads would validate the channel.
