# ECONOMICS

If Credex deployed this tool publicly, the economics could work surprisingly well because AI infrastructure costs are already large and growing for startups.

Assume the average qualified startup customer spends around $2,000/month on AI tooling and infrastructure. If Credex helps reduce costs through discounted credits and takes a margin of 15%, the monthly revenue per converted customer would be:

$2,000 × 15% = $300/month

Annualized, each converted customer could be worth approximately:

$300 × 12 = $3,600/year

Now consider acquisition costs.

For organic channels like Reddit, Hacker News, X, and founder communities, CAC could remain relatively low because the tool itself is useful and shareable.

Estimated CAC by channel:
- Reddit/community distribution: $20–40
- Founder DMs/manual outreach: $50–80
- Product Hunt launch traffic: $30–60
- Organic SEO over time: <$20

Suppose 1,000 users complete audits monthly.

If:
- 20% submit email → 200 leads
- 10% of leads book consultation → 20 calls
- 25% of consultations convert → 5 paying customers

Then monthly recurring revenue added becomes:

5 × $300 = $1,500 MRR added per month

Over 18 months, with improving conversion and compounding organic traffic, this becomes meaningful.

To reach $1M ARR:
$1,000,000 / 12 = ~$83,000 MRR needed

At $300/month average revenue per customer:
$83,000 / $300 ≈ 277 active customers

That is achievable if the tool consistently drives high-intent startup leads.

For example:
- 25,000 monthly visitors
- 5,000 completed audits
- 1,000 captured leads
- 100 consultations
- 15–20 new customers/month

The biggest assumption is that audit recommendations are genuinely valuable and trustworthy. If users feel the tool is biased or inaccurate, conversion rates collapse.

The product works economically because the lead quality is unusually high. Users completing an AI spend audit are already signaling purchase intent around AI infrastructure optimization.
